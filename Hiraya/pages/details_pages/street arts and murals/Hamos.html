Hamos

Description: "Hamos", a Bicol term meaning “Join us!”, is a mural advocating for good governance and people’s participation in societal change. Painted on a 9-feet by 40-feet vacant wall along a highway in Barangay Basud, it serves as a call to action for civic engagement and amplifies the demand for human rights and democracy.

Title: Hamos

Artist: 1Sambayan-Sorsogon Youth, RJ Abrigo (Kurit-Lagting), Gilbert Catabian, and other volunteer artists.

Year Created: 2021

Medium/Material Used:Paint on concrete wall

Dimension/Size: 9 feet x 40 feet.

Location: Barangay Basud, Sorsogon, Philippines.

Theme: Civic engagement, good governance, human rights, and social responsibility.

Significance in Filipino Culture:

The mural symbolizes the Filipino people's ongoing struggle for transparency, accountability, and active participation in governance. It reflects the collective efforts of artists and local communities in using art as a medium for political expression and social change.

Facts / Trivia:

  • The mural was supported by local residents who donated food, paints, and other materials. It highlights the importance of community-driven activism and the role of art in amplifying social and political discourse in the Philippines.
