The Happiest Place on Earth

Elmer Borlongan





Details

  • Title: The Happiest Place on Earth
  • Artist: Elmer Borlongan
  • Year Created: 2017
  • Dimension/Size:91.5 x 122 cm (36 x 48 inches)
  • Location:Pintô Art Museum in Antipolo City, Rizal, Philippines

Artwork Description

The painting portrays a solitary figure, cloaked in a somber garment with only the face and hands visible, consuming a serving of French fries. The fries are presented in packaging reminiscent of a popular Filipino fast-food chain, creating a striking contrast between the austere appearance of the individual and the indulgent nature of the food.

Theme

Borlongan's work often delves into the everyday experiences of Filipinos, highlighting social issues and the resilience of the human spirit. In The Happiest Place on Earth, he captures the paradox of fast-food culture within a developing nation, where such establishments symbolize both modernity and the stark disparities in wealth and access to resources.
