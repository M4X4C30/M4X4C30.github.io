El Asesinato del Gobernador Bustamante

Félix Resurrección Hidalgo





Details

  • Title: El Asesinato del Gobernador Bustamante
  • Artist: Félix Resurrección Hidalgo
  • Year Created: Late 19th century
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 412 x 338 cm
  • Location:National Museum of Fine Arts, Manila, Philippines

Artwork Description

El Asesinato del Gobernador Bustamante (The Assassination of Governor Bustamante) is a significant painting by Félix Resurrección Hidalgo, depicting the brutal murder of Spanish Governor-General Fernando Manuel de Bustamante y Bustillo in 1719. The scene unfolds in the old Governor's Palace in Intramuros, Manila, where an enraged mob, led by friars, violently attacks the governor. Hidalgo masterfully captures the tension and chaos of the moment, using dramatic lighting, expressive brushstrokes, and a dynamic composition.

Theme

The painting explores themes of injustice, power struggles, and the conflict between civil authority and the Church. It symbolizes a critical historical event where Spanish colonial rule and religious influence clashed violently, highlighting themes of political corruption and resistance.
