Las Domas Romanas

Juan Luna





Details

  • Title: Las Damas Romanas
  • Artist: Juan Luna
  • Year Created: 1882
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 100 cm x 170 cm (39 in x 67 in)
  • Location:Private Collection/Auction House

Artwork Description

The artwork depicts a group of elegantly dressed women in a Roman-inspired setting, showcasing Luna’s mastery of light, shadow, and intricate details. The painting reflects European influences, particularly in its composition and style, while also highlighting Luna’s ability to capture grace and sophistication in his subjects.

Theme

The theme of Las Damas Romanas revolves around elegance, femininity, and cultural influence. It highlights the grace and sophistication of women in a Roman-inspired setting.

Facts / Trivia

Las Damas Romanas was painted while Luna was a student of the Spanish Academy in Rome.For a long period, the painting was considered lost until it was rediscovered and eventually auctioned at Christie's.
