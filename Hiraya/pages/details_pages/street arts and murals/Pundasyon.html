Pundasyon (Ang Supremo at ang Rajah)

Description: “Pundasyon (Ang Supremo at ang Rajah)” by Gerilya, the mural shows a portrait of Rajah Sulayman, the pre-Hispanic King of Manila

Title:Pundasyon (Ang Supremo at ang Rajah)

Artist: Gerilya

Year Created: 2022

Medium/Material Used:Spray Paint and Acrylic on Concrete

Dimension/Size: 20-meter-high columns (spanning ten underpass columns)

Location:Nagtahan Bridge, Metro Manila, Philippines

Theme:National Identity, Resistance, and Heritage

Significance in Filipino Culture:

The mural bridges the past and present, reminding Filipinos of their rich history and the struggles their ancestors faced in shaping the nation. It promotes historical awareness and civic pride while reclaiming urban spaces for artistic and cultural expression.

Facts / Trivia:

  • The mural is part of the Urban Artscape Project, which aims to integrate Philippine history into urban landscapes.
  • Rajah Sulayman symbolizes pre-colonial Manila’s sovereignty, while Bonifacio represents the revolutionary spirit against Spanish rule.
