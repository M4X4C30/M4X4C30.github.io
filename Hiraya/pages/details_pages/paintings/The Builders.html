The Builders

Victorio Edades





Details

  • Title: The Builders
  • Artist: Victorio Edades
  • Year Created: 1928
  • Dimension/Size:182 cm (height) x 321 cm (width)
  • Location:Cultural Center of the Philippines

Artwork Description

The painting depicts laborers engaged in hard work, showing gritty realism with dark tones and rough brushstrokes, emphasizing the struggles of the working class.

Theme

The painting reflects themes of hard labor, social realism, and the dignity of the working class. It challenges traditional idealized Filipino art by showing the harsh realities of laborers and their contributions to nation-building.

Significance in Filipino Culture

The Builders is a landmark painting in Philippine modern art, as it challenged the traditional beauty standards in art and introduced a new perspective on Filipino identity. By depicting the hardworking laborers, Edades gave importance to the working class and their contributions to society, making the painting a symbol of social realism and nationalism in the Philippines.

Facts / Trivia

Edades introduced modernism to Philippine art, breaking away from the bright and idealized rural scenes of his time.
