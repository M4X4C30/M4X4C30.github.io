Dalagang Bukid

Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto





Details

  • Title: Dalagang Bukid
  • Artist: Fernando Amorsolo
  • Year Created: 1919
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 85 x 59.5 cm (33.5 x 23.4 in)
  • Location:National Museum of the Philippines in Gallery IX of the Early 20th Century Philippine Portrait Hall
  • Awards: Silver medal at the 1904 World's Fair in Saint Louis, Missouri

Artwork Description

“Dalagang Bukid" is a beautiful portrayal of a young Filipino woman in a rural setting. The woman is depicted in traditional Filipino attire, standing gracefully amidst the lush countryside, with the bright sunlight casting a warm glow on her face. The painting captures the simplicity and beauty of rural Filipino life, symbolizing the purity and charm of the Filipino woman.

Theme

It revolves in the beauty of Filipino rural life and the grace of the Filipino woman and it highlights the simplicity, innocence, and purity

Significance in Filipino Culture

It represents the idealized image of a Filipina woman, particularly a young rural maiden, embodying beauty, innocence, and the traditional values associated with provincial life

Facts / Trivia

The title "Dalagang Bukid" (which means “Maiden of the Fields”) refers to the romanticized image of rural women in Filipino literature and culture
