The Parisian Life

Juan Novicio Luna





Details

  • Title: The Parisian Life
  • Artist: Juan Luna
  • Year Created: 1892
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size:57 centimeters by 79 centimeters (22 inches by 31 inches).
  • Location:National Museum of Fine Arts in Manila, Philippines
  • Awards:The Parisian Life by Juan Luna won a silver medal at the 1904 World's Fair in Saint Louis, Missouri. This was the only time the painting was exhibited to the public.

Artwork Description

“The Parisian Life” portrays a young woman sitting in a café, dressed in a fashionable light pink dress, with a somewhat melancholic or contemplative expression. The background features three men seated together, engaged in conversation. These men are believed to be Juan Luna, José Rizal, and Ariston Bautista Lin, all key figures in the Philippine nationalist movement.

Theme

A complex portrayal of contemporary social norms, gender politics, and national allegory .

Significance in Filipino Culture

The Government Service Insurance System (GSIS) bought the painting for ₱46 million in 2002, bringing it back to the Philippines. One of the men in the painting, Ariston Bautista Lin, bought the artwork from Luna and kept it in his home for decades.

Facts / Trivia

Considered one of Manansala's most important works, reflecting the social realities of the Philippines after World War II
