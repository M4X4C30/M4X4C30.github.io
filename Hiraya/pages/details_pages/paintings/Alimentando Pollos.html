Alimentando Pollos (Feeding Chickens)

Simón Flores y de la Rosa





Details

  • Title: Alimentando Pollos (Feeding Chickens)
  • Artist:Simón Flores y de la Rosa/li>
  • Year Created: 1896
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 41 x 51 cm. (16.1 x 20.1 in.)
  • Location:National Museum of Fine Arts, Manila, Philippines

Artwork Description

This artwork portrays a serene scene of a Filipino woman feeding chickens in a tranquil provincial setting. Flores masterfully captures the simplicity and warmth of everyday rural life, reflecting the harmonious relationship between humans and nature in the Philippines.

Theme

The painting emphasizes themes of rural simplicity, daily life, and the integral role of women in agricultural communities. It showcases the peaceful coexistence between people and their environment, highlighting traditional practices that have been part of Filipino culture for generations

Significance in Filipino Culture

This painting is significant because it preserves the image of traditional Filipino rural life, before modernization changed agricultural practices. It reflects the hardworking spirit of Filipinos, particularly women, and serves as a visual record of pre-colonial customs and household activities.

Facts / Trivia

Alimentando Pollos has been declared a National Treasure by the National Museum of the Philippines, underscoring its cultural and historical significance.
