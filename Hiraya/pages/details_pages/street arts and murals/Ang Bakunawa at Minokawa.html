Ang Bakunawa at Minokawa

Description: Ang Bakunawa at Minokawa is a striking mural by the artist collective Gerilya, featuring two legendary creatures from Filipino mythology—the Bakunawa, a serpent-like dragon believed to devour the moon, and the Minokawa, a giant eagle that was said to swallow the sun.

Title:Ang Bakunawa at Minokawa

Artist: Gerilya

Year Created: 2021

Medium/Material Used:Spray Paint and Acrylic on Concrete

Dimension/Size: Large-scale mural

Location: Intramuros, Manila

Theme: Mythology, Cosmic Balance, and Filipino Folklore

Significance in Filipino Culture:

This mural revives and popularizes ancient Filipino legends, encouraging cultural appreciation and reconnecting Filipinos with their pre-colonial beliefs. It also highlights the importance of storytelling and oral traditions, preserving indigenous myths in a contemporary artistic format.

Facts / Trivia:

  • The Bakunawa is often linked to lunar eclipses, while the Minokawa is associated with solar eclipses in ancient Filipino beliefs. Gerilya is known for its nationalist and historical themes, blending street art, comics, and graffiti. This mural is part of the collective’s larger mission to reclaim public spaces for cultural storytelling.
