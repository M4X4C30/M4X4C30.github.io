Calamansi & Sampaguita

Description: The artwork features the calamansi plant and the sampaguita flower, both significant in Filipino culture. Set against a black background, the mural uses pastel colors to make the native flora stand out, symbolizing local traditions preserved through centuries

Title: Calamansi & Sampagauita

Artist: Pastel (Francisco Diaz)

Year Created: 2016

Medium/Material Used:Paint on concrete wall

Location: Bonifacio Global City Corporate Center, 30th Street corner 11th Avenue, Taguig City, Philippines

Theme: Cultural heritage, resilience, and the evolution of native symbolism in the Philippines.

Significance in Filipino Culture:

The mural reflects on the Philippines' history of colonization since 1565 and its continuous efforts to preserve native traditions. The calamansi is traditionally used in Filipino funerals for purification, while the sampaguita, the national flower, symbolizes humility and reverence. In contemporary times, sampaguita garlands are often sold by children on the streets, highlighting social and economic challenges.

Facts / Trivia:

  • Pastel, whose real name is Francisco Diaz, is known for incorporating floral elements into urban settings, aiming to connect city environments with nature.
