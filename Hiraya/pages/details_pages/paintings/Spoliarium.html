Spoliarium

Juan Novicio Luna





Details

  • Title: Spoliarium
  • Artist: Juan Luna
  • Year Created: 11884
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 4.22 meters (13.8 feet) in height and 7.675 meters (25.2 feet) in width.
  • Location: National Museum of Fine Arts in Manila, Philippines
  • Awards: Juan Luna's painting Spoliarium won a gold medal at the 1884 Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes in Madrid

Artwork Description

"Spoliarium" is a painting by Filipino artist Juan Luna, depicting a scene from a Roman circus where dead gladiators are being stripped of their weapons and garments after a fight, highlighting the brutality and suffering of the defeated

Theme

Oppression, Injustice, and Tragic Fate

Significance in Filipino Culture

Represents the suffering and exploitation of the Filipino people under Spanish colonial rule.

Facts / Trivia

It was kept kept in Spain for a long time before being gifted to the Philippines by the Spanish government in 1958. It is now considered a national treasure and is prominently displayed at the National Museum of Fine Arts in Manila.
