The Fisherman

Ang Kiukok





Details

  • Title:The Fisherman
  • Artist: Ang Kiukok
  • Year Created:1981
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 40” x 80” (102 cm x 202 cm)
  • Location:Private Auction House

Artwork Description

The painting portrays three fishermen engaged in the strenuous task of hauling a net brimming with fish, symbolizing both the struggles and hopes inherent in their daily lives. The dynamic composition and vivid colors reflect the intensity and resilience of the human spirit, themes recurrent in Kiukok's body of work.

Theme

The theme of The Fishermen by Ang Kiukok revolves around struggle, survival, and resilience.

Significance in Filipino Culture

It captures the realities of livelihood in the Philippines, where many depend on fishing as a means of survival, showcasing both the hardships and hopes of daily life.

Facts / Trivia

In 2001, Ang Kiukok was honored as a National Artist for Visual Arts in the Philippines, acknowledging his significant contributions to the country's art scene.
