Tayo ang Gatilyo

Description: The artwork features the faces of indigenous individuals marked with red laser targets on their foreheads, symbolizing the systemic persecution and displacement they endure from their ancestral lands. This visual representation sheds light on the struggles of indigenous communities in the Philippines, emphasizing the urgent need for societal awareness and action.

Title: Tayo ang Gatilyo

Artist: Archie Oclos

Year Created: 2017

Medium/Material Used:Spray Paint and Acrylic on Wall

Location: Jupiter Street, Makati City, Philippines

Theme: Collective Responsibility, Indigenous Struggle,Systemic Violence

Significance in Filipino Culture:

In the context of Filipino culture, "Tayo ang Gatilyo" holds profound significance as it amplifies the voices of indigenous peoples, who are often overlooked despite their integral role in the nationâ€™s history and identity. The Philippines is home to numerous indigenous groups, collectively known as the Lumad in Mindanao and other tribal communities across the archipelago, whose lands and traditions have been threatened by modernization, militarization, and capitalist expansion. Oclos, a Manila-based artist with roots in Catanduanes, draws from his own background and education at the University of the Philippines to serve the country through art, a value deeply embedded in Filipino artistic tradition.
