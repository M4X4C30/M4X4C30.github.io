Planting Rice

Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto





Details

  • Title: Planting Rice
  • Artist: Fernando Amorsolo
  • Year Created: 1921 - 1944
  • Medium/Material Used: Oil on Canvas
  • Dimension/Size: 28 in x 39.8 in (71.1 cm x 101 cm)
  • Location:Galeria De las Islas in Intramuros, Manila and the National Museum of Fine Arts.

Artwork Description

A renowned painting depicting a scene of Filipino farmers working in a rice paddy, often with the iconic Mayon Volcano in the background, showcasing the artist's signature use of light and color to portray a serene and idyllic rural landscape, highlighting the communal effort involved in rice cultivation and the beauty of Filipino countryside life.

Theme

It depicts the idealized rural life and culture of the Philippines

Significance in Filipino Culture

It celebrates the hard work and resilience of Filipino farmers, who are the backbone of the country’s agricultural economy. The painting reflects the importance of rice farming as a staple in Filipino life and its role in the nation’s food security and cultural identity. It also honors the connection between Filipinos and the land, highlighting the themes of community, cooperation, and tradition.

Facts / Trivia

"Planting Rice" is not a single painting, but a series created by Amorsolo across several years, showcasing different variations of the rice planting scene.
